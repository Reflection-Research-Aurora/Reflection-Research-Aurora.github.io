# CS × Research Program for High School Students

Welcome to the **CS × Research Program**, a structured mentorship curriculum designed for high school students aiming for competitive university programs in Computer Science, Engineering, Life Sciences, and Pre-Med.

In today's admissions landscape — Waterloo CS/Engineering, U of T Life Sciences, McMaster Health Sciences — high grades alone are not enough. Admissions officers are looking for one critical signal: **Can you use technology to solve real problems, and can you explain exactly how you did it?**

This program trains students to become computational thinkers who apply CS skills to real domains — biology, health, environment, or any field that matters to them. Through a structured three-tier progression, students move from writing their first line of Python to producing independent, portfolio-grade research work.

---

## Core Philosophy

### Authentic Research, Not Packaged Credentials

We strictly oppose ghostwriting and artificially assembled "research" portfolios. Here, students work with real data, face real failures, and build real understanding:

- **No toy datasets.** Every project uses publicly available, domain-relevant data. Bugs, non-converging models, and messy data are part of the process — and exactly the kind of story a strong university essay is built around.
- **100% cloud-based.** Everything runs on Google Colab or equivalent cloud environments. No local setup headaches. Open a browser and start building.
- **CS + Any Domain.** This is not a bioinformatics-only program. Students apply computational methods to whatever domain is most aligned with their interests and university goals — health data, environmental science, social systems, sports analytics, or beyond.

### Research Communication Policy

A student who genuinely understands their own project is far more persuasive than one who doesn't. Our goal is to make sure you can speak clearly and confidently about your work in any setting — an interview, an essay prompt, or a conversation with an admissions officer.

**What we do:** Throughout the program, your mentor coaches you on how to document your process, structure your findings, and articulate the decisions you made and why. This is a transferable skill that makes every piece of writing you produce more convincing.

**What we do not do:** We do not draft, co-write, edit, or review university application essays or supplementary materials. Your words must be your own — and this matters beyond ethics. Waterloo AIF and U of T supplementary evaluators are experienced readers. An essay that doesn't match your voice, or a project you hesitate to explain under pressure, will hurt far more than it helps. We build the depth of understanding that makes your own writing genuinely strong.

## The 3-Tier Progression

### Level 1: Foundations — Python, AI Tools & ML Basics (10 Weeks)

**Target:** Grade 9–10 students with zero or minimal coding background.  
**Goal:** Build confident, practical coding ability and a working understanding of how machine learning works.

We teach coding as a tool for thinking, not as syntax to memorize.

**What you learn:**
- **Python fundamentals** — variables, control flow, functions, and debugging basics
- **Vibe coding with AI tools** — how to use AI coding assistants (such as GitHub Copilot or Claude) to accelerate development, and critically, how to read, validate, and correct AI-generated code rather than blindly accepting it. This is a core professional skill in modern software development.
- **Data literacy** — handling and visualizing data with Pandas and Matplotlib/Seaborn
- **ML basics** — how classification and regression models work conceptually; hands-on practice with scikit-learn (Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, Random Forest); reading and interpreting model outputs

**What you build:**  
A guided end-to-end mini project: loading a real public health dataset, exploring it visually, training a baseline model, and explaining the result in plain language. Example: predicting cardiovascular disease risk from health indicators.

**Core takeaway:** You leave with functional Python skills, familiarity with AI-assisted development, and a first taste of what it means to use data to answer a question.

---

### Level 2: Applied CS × Domain — Structured Problem Solving (12–16 Weeks)

**Target:** Grade 10–11 students with Python experience, or graduates of Level 1.  
**Goal:** Develop real problem-solving skill and produce a portfolio piece through two structured, competition-style ML projects.  
**Format:** Small cohort, limited to 3–4 students. Weekly sessions plus async support between sessions.

This is the core program. Rather than a single open-ended project, students work through **two structured ML problems** drawn from public platforms such as Kaggle, DrivenData, or similar competitions. Each problem has a real dataset, a defined objective, and a public evaluation benchmark — giving students a concrete, honest measure of their work.

**How it runs:**

- **Problem 1 (Weeks 1–6):** A structured entry-level ML competition problem, selected to match the cohort's skill level and interest domain. Focus is on the full pipeline: data exploration, feature engineering, model selection, evaluation, and iteration. Students learn what it actually means to improve a model — not just run it once.
- **Problem 2 (Weeks 7–14):** A more complex problem of the student's choosing, ideally in a domain relevant to their university goals (health data, environmental data, economic indicators, etc.). Students take greater ownership of methodology decisions and document their reasoning throughout.
- **Buffer & Wrap-up (Weeks 15–16):** Presentation preparation and report writing.

**Domain flexibility:** Students are encouraged to choose domains that connect to their broader interests. The CS skills transfer; the domain context strengthens the story in university applications.

**Deliverables:**
1. Two documented project notebooks with written explanations of methodology and results
2. An Academic Research Report (structured like a research paper: introduction, methods, results, discussion)
3. A professional-style Poster Presentation suitable for school showcases or science fairs

**Duration note:** 12-week cohorts move through both problems at a faster pace with stronger prior coding background. 16-week cohorts allow more iteration time and domain exploration. Your mentor will clarify at the assessment call.

### Level 3: VIP Capstone — Original CS × Research (16+ Weeks, 1-on-1)

**Target:** Highly motivated students with strong technical foundations. Admittance by assessment only.  
**Goal:** Produce original, independently defensible research work targeting credible external recognition.  
**Format:** Fully 1-on-1 mentorship. This is not a class; it is a research collaboration.

At this level, we are not teaching. We are doing research together.

**What the mentorship covers:**

- **Research question design** — Identifying an original, underexplored question at the intersection of CS and the student's chosen domain. This is the hardest part of real research, and we work through it together rigorously.
- **End-to-end implementation** — Data acquisition, cleaning, pipeline design, model development, ablation studies, and result interpretation. The standard is graduate-student-level rigor, not high school project quality.
- **Writing and presentation** — Producing a manuscript-quality report and preparing for public presentation. Feedback is direct and iterative, the way academic mentorship actually works.

**Target outcomes (tiered by readiness and progress):**

| Tier | Target |
|------|--------|
| Entry | Local or regional student science showcase (e.g., regional science fair feeding into CWSF) |
| Intermediate | STEM Fellowship Big Data Challenge; other national Canadian student competitions |
| Advanced | Submission to entry-level peer-reviewed journals: *Journal of Emerging Investigators*, *PLOS ONE* (IF ~3.7), *BMC Bioinformatics* (IF ~3.0), *Computers* — MDPI (IF ~2.6), *Frontiers in* relevant field (IF ~2.5–4) |

Being realistic: journal submission is a goal, not a guarantee. A well-executed, clearly written project with a defined scope is a stronger application asset than a rushed submission to a low-credibility venue. We target journals where quality is the admission criterion — not payment.

### Level X: Custom Pathway — Fully Tailored (Duration TBD)

**Target:** Students whose goals, timelines, or academic profiles do not fit neatly into a standard cohort.  
**Goal:** Build a program around the student rather than fitting the student into a fixed template.

This is the right option for students with unusual academic timelines, hybrid goals across multiple fields (e.g., CS + music, CS + linguistics), or needs that span both technical mentorship and broader academic support.

The pathway begins with a detailed consultation to map the student's current skills, upcoming deadlines, and priority goals. From there, content, pacing, and session structure are designed from scratch and can adjust as the student progresses.

Level X can also serve as an entry point for families who are uncertain which standard level is the right fit.

## Why Choose This Program?

1. **No assembly-line credentials.** We don't guarantee publication or competition placement as a deliverable you're paying for. We build the genuine technical depth and project ownership that makes your work defensible anywhere — in an essay, in an interview, in front of a panel. That's what actually differentiates a strong application.

2. **CS + any domain, not just one field.** The skills you build (data handling, ML modeling, research writing) apply across disciplines. Whether your interest is medicine, environmental policy, economics, or something else entirely, the program adapts.

3. **Ontario-aware mentorship.** The program is built with awareness of what Waterloo, U of T, and McMaster actually evaluate: evidence of self-directed learning, complex problem-solving, and the ability to reflect on failure and iteration. That's the loop every project here puts students through.

4. **Expert-led.** Mentorship is led by an active practitioner with direct experience in computational research and machine learning. Students work with methods that are used in real research settings, not simplified approximations.

## Program Logistics

- **Sessions:** Weekly live sessions (60–90 min) per level, online.
- **Async support:** Between sessions, students have access to a dedicated channel (Discord or equivalent) for questions, debugging help, and feedback on in-progress work. Response time is within 24 hours on weekdays.
- **Tools:** Google Colab (free tier covers Levels 1–2). Level 3 students doing extended training runs may need Colab Pro (~$13 CAD/month) — this will be flagged in advance if relevant.
- **Level X duration:** Determined at the initial consultation based on student goals and timeline.
