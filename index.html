Students gain hands-on research experience, develop technical expertise, and build competitive profiles for leading universities and scholarship opportunities worldwide.

The program is CS × any domain, not limited to biology or health. Students have worked on projects in health data, environmental science, sports analytics, social systems, and more. The computational methods (data handling, machine learning modeling, research writing) transfer across fields. Students are encouraged to choose a domain that connects to their university goals and genuine interests, since that connection comes through clearly in application essays and interviews.

Level 3 follows a defined research progression (16+ weeks) with clear phase milestones and targets credible external outputs. Level X is for students whose goals, timelines, or academic profiles do not fit that structure. Level X is also a good entry point if you are unsure which standard level fits best.

Life happens. Contact us as early as possible and we will work out a reasonable arrangement. For Level 2 cohort programs, mid-program withdrawals affect the small group dynamic, so we ask for at least two weeks' notice where possible. For Level 3, the deposit is non-refundable as it reserves a block of mentor time, but remaining installment payments are handled on a case-by-case basis depending on how far the project has progressed.

Our mentors are tenure-track faculty at Greater Toronto Area universities. They are active researchers with institutional affiliations, not tutoring agency contractors. Students in Level 2 receive a formal reference letter upon completion describing their project, performance, and demonstrated capabilities. Students in Level 3 receive a strong reference letter with significantly more detailed: it speaks to the student's research aptitude, intellectual curiosity, resilience, and specific original contributions observed over 16+ weeks of 1-on-1 work. A letter from a faculty research collaborator carries considerably more weight than a program completion note, and is the type of letter graduate programs and scholarship committees find most compelling.
